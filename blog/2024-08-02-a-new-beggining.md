# Who am I & What is My Purpose?

Hi! My name is wander-mind. I am a System Administrator and a Linux enthusiast from Bulgaria, aspiring one day to be an acceptable computer software programmer. I have been using computers since we first got our family computer, and since then I have been fascinated with them. Back then, as a 5-6 year old, with fundamental knowledge of English, I saw for the first time a machine that could store and play all your favorite music and movies, where you could write, format, and distribute all of your fancy documents and books, and browse the internet when it was actually still free as the wild west with all the beautiful amateur blogs and websites, with GIFs as the background and neon text, and play all the pirated games you desired. Beautiful!

Since then, a lot has changed. Facebook came along, and I hate everything about it, social media in general. Forums are disappearing bit by bit, personal blogs and websites, some based on the imagination of the possibility, some based on fascination of the infinite wit, using all their imagination despite the technology's limitations, creating a world from their own curiosity. But as they fade, we start to see a digital era changing constantly, where voices once unique and free now blend into a vast sea. Now everybody thinks they are free, brave, unique, righteous, and important, but they are actually just clones of themselves, molded by big corporations to consume and vote for whatever and whoever they say, without any critical thinking. FFS, they can't even remember their Instagram password when switching to their new fancy phone they saw from an ad. Good thing everything is "safe, easy, secure, and private," or so they say. Fuck them!

Despite all the robotification, I still love this "Brave New World"! I wish to help build good software that gives power to people, but is also good enough to sell. I want to help make that software useful, featureful, secure, and fun. I want to become a programmer!

I have finished high school, specializing in math and computer science. I have also finished university as a computer and software engineer. I have plans to get my master's degree too, just for the hell of it. I think it will look good on my CV. In high school, university, work, and the Software University of Sofia, where I am getting certified as a QA engineer now, I have learned many things, like C++, C#, website building, networks, servers, cybersecurity, and virtual machines, but all on a fundamental level, I would say, based on my critical standards. I am not great in any of these fields. I have only learned what I have needed so far. But now, since I have grown up and have real plans for myself, I desire to become the beast I could have become if I had really put the effort into it.

Now, I am not one who claims I can become a master at your craft just with some online courses, or with some 5-month bootcamp, or with my CS degree. No, it takes maybe 10,000 hours, or 10 years, to really become good as a software problem solver. It takes some knowledge in all kinds of fields to begin with: computer history, how the hardware works on the smallest level, how computers connect and how networks work, setting up a server, writing a website, creating a big project like a game or text editor, decompilation, information security, disaster recovery, protection against script-kiddies who use Nikto, Metasploit, and Burp Suite, actually learning how to program, making your code efficient, and writing proper tests to prevent bugs, and so much more. Here, on this GitHub page of mine, I will start over. And yes - I used an LLM to fix my grammar, I'm not a native tea-sipper.
